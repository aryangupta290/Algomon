Challenge (Knapsack Problem ) :

You have a match against the Greedy Man Johto . Your task is to learn a certain set of dp algorithms in a given amount of time but there is one twist , you will have to compete with Johto .

Both of you start learning the algorithms together using different (or same ) techniques and whoever ends up the the maximum learning_experience after the given time will be allowed to train with sensei Shiro . You obviously don't want to miss this opportunity and hence want to choose a technique that will allow you to get maximum amount of learning_experience so that you can win against Shiro and have a chance to train with the great Shiro.

You have studied how Johto used to select algorithms in the past and found that he finds the ratio of

learning_experience_of_the algorithm/ time_to_learn_that_algorithm

and starts picking the algorithms with the maximum value of this ratio until he runs out of time . So your job is to find a better way to choose them .

Their are 5 algorithms to choose from and they are as follows :

Algorithm No. Learning_Experience Time to learn this algorithm

1 6 1

2 12 3

3 10 2

The total time allotted to learn these algorithms is 5 hours.

Let us think of the maximum learning experience Johto can accumulate in this time . So let us call the ratio of experience/ time for any algorithm by r . So r1=6;r2=4;r3=5r_1=6;r_2=4;r_3=5r1​=6;r2​=4;r3​=5﻿ .

So greedily Johto will first choose to learn the first algorithm .

Now, after leaning the first algorithm he will have total_experience of 6 and will have 4 hours left on the clock . Now he will learn the third algorithm cause it has the next highest value of the ratio r .

After learning second algorithm , Johto will have total experience points of 16 and will have 2 hours left on the clock but in this time he won't be able to complete the 2nd Algorithm .

Hence by the end Johto would have accumulated a total of 16 experience points .

Let us to try to see if we can try to do any better and yes we will try to use dynamic programming to see if we can do any better .

Our first task should be to construct a DAG of sub-problems . So our basic question was if say if we can have total experience E in the given time (5 hrs) .Sub-problems can be like if we can have a Experience E' in time t' (which is less than 5) . We can now think of ways of how to use the answers to already calculated sub-problems to find the answer for a bigger problem in time time .

Now let us try to formally write what we have thought so for , we will construct a dp[][]dp[][]dp[][]﻿ table where dp[i][j]dp[i][j]dp[i][j]﻿ will denote the maximum experience we can get considering the algorithms from index 1 to i and the total time we have is j .

We can now start to think about transitions . When we are at the i'th Algorithm , we may either learn it or not . If we learn the algorithm (considering we have sufficient time in our hand to learn this algorithm) , then we will get the experience points for that algorithm and the total time left will be j - t , where t is the time needed to learn the i'th algorithm. So we can use the answer of the smaller sub-problem ( max experience we can get from first i-1 algorithms considering we have j-t time .

Else if we reject the i'th algorithm , then the answer would simply be the maximum experience we can get from the first i-1 algorithms in j time .

So our answer for current state (i,j) would simply be :

dp[i][j]=max(dp[i−1][j],dp[i−1][j−t]+e)dp[i][j]=max(dp[i-1][j],dp[i-1][j-t]+e)dp[i][j]=max(dp[i−1][j],dp[i−1][j−t]+e)﻿

where e,t are the experience gained and time needed to learn the i'th algorithm .
